About our Cookies

Chocolate Chip
Chocolate chip cookies are drop cookies with chocolate chip toppings that can be soft and doughy or crisp and crispy.
Gingersnaps
This well-known holiday treat has the ideal balance of sweetness and spiciness thanks to ginger, cinnamon, molasses, and powdered nutmeg spices.
Oatmeal Raisin
Oatmeal raisin cookies are another variety of drop cookies that are terribly underappreciated. Brown sugar, raisins, and oatmeal make up the dough. They bring back memories of simpler times and grandma's home and are warm and comfortable.
Peanut Butter
A favorite peanut butter fan, these cookies are prepared with hand-rolled dough, often flattened with a fork to create a recognizable waffle pattern.
Whoopee Pie
Two soft, pillowy cookies are combined with a marshmallow filling to create these sandwich-style sweets. Whoopie pies were first sold in Maine and are now exceedingly well-liked. Baker traditionally made Whoopie pies with chocolate biscuits.
Sugar Cookies
Everyone like sugar cookies, but few people can honestly say they are their favorite. It's kind of like vanilla ice cream or cookies. This ordinary cookie comprises simple ingredients, including sugar, flour, butter, eggs, and vanilla.
Biscotti
The term "biscotti" literally translates to "twice-cooked/baked" since biscotti are baked twice, giving them a firm texture and a long shelf life. Its origins may be traced to ancient Rome, which served as a practical snack for travelers.
Spritz Cookies
Spritz cookies are made by squirting the dough through a cookie press to create intricate designs. Various flavors and colors, including almond, peanut butter, orange, chocolate, and others, are available in spritz cookies. For a festive treat, dunk them in chocolate and sprinkle on some decorations.
Pinwheel Cookies
The holiday season is the ideal time to make these gorgeous pinwheel cookies! These cookies are simple to make and sweet and buttery. These cookies will steal the show's captivating swirls of vanilla and chocolate!
Butter Cookies
Butter cookies feature a high butter-to-flour ratio, similar to shortbread cookies. The difference is that butter cookies have more sugar and flour than shortbread, which helps them maintain their shape better.
